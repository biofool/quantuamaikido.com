about Richard Moon

6th Dan Aikido Sensei International Peace Negotiator Founder, Extraordinary Listening

I never set out to write a book about quantum physics.

I'm an Aikido teacher. Sixth Dan, if titles matter. Student of Robert Nadeau, who studied with O Sensei—Morihei Ueshiba, founder of Aikido. I've spent decades on the mat, learning what happens when someone attacks you with real intent, real energy, real consequences.

But the mat teaches more than technique.

In the mid-1990s, I found myself in Cyprus. Not as a tourist. As part of a team attempting track two diplomacy between Greek and Turkish Cypriots—communities divided by decades of mistrust, pain, history. Our job: bring twenty people from each side together. Create space for conversation where none existed.

The first night, I walked into a room of exhausted facilitators arguing about the next day's agenda. Five of us had been planning for hours. Nobody agreed. Everyone was cranky. The energy was wrong.

I said something like, "If you look at the totality, what's wrong points to what's right. Envision tomorrow if it were great."

Then I laughed. "Of course, I could be wrong. Anything can happen."

The room changed.

That's when I understood: the principles I'd been practicing on the mat for decades worked everywhere. Not just in dojos. Not just when someone grabs your wrist or throws a punch. They work in conference rooms. In difficult conversations. In the moment you realize your teenager is lying to you. In the split second before you say something you'll regret.

They work in the space between stimulus and response.

the electronic coaching years

Long before this book, there was voicemail.

1997. I was doing executive coaching for corporate clients. But not the usual way—not sitting in offices talking about leadership frameworks. Instead, I'd leave 60-120 second voicemail messages. Coaching delivered as voice, as breath, as immediate practical wisdom someone could absorb during their commute.

Thousands of these messages. Each one exploring what I now call WIGO—What Is Really Going On beneath the surface. Teaching people to find the pause between trigger and reaction. Showing them how to transform pressure into presence.

I called it "the swimming pool with a message at the bottom." You had to dive down, feel the pressure, retrieve the wisdom.

That corpus of work—all those short teachings—turned out to perfectly match how people consume content today. YouTube shorts. Instagram reels. TikTok.

The format we use now? I was doing it thirty years ago. Not because I was prescient. Because truth has its own timing.

what aikido taught me

Aikido is commonly described as a Japanese martial art.

That's not quite right.

O Sensei called Aikido "the realization of love." He described it as transcending the paradigm of martial arts the way quantum mechanics transcended classical physics, the way jazz transcended composition.

On the mat, you learn this:

When someone attacks, you have a choice. You can resist—meet force with force, create collision, make enemies. Or you can do something else entirely.

You can enter. Blend. Harmonize with the energy of the attack itself.

Not by being weak. Not by giving up. By being so present, so centered, so connected to your own core that you can feel the attacker's intention before it becomes action. You move with them, not against them. You redirect the energy. Transform it.

Conflict isn't something to win or lose.
It's energy waiting to be transformed.

That's Aiki. That's harmony. That's the sword that cuts things together instead of apart.

the quantum connection

I'm not a physicist. I've never worked in a lab or published in scientific journals.

But I noticed something.

The principles O Sensei taught—principles I was teaching executives, applying in peace negotiations, living on the mat—they echo what quantum mechanics describes.

Wave-particle duality. The observer effect. Superposition. Uncertainty. The unified field.

These aren't just physics concepts. They're descriptions of how consciousness works. How attention shapes reality. How the quality of your presence determines what becomes possible.

In quantum mechanics, the observer affects what's observed. How you look at something changes what you see.

In Aikido, your state determines what you experience. When you're centered, attacks become opportunities. When you're reactive, even kindness feels threatening.

Same principle. Different laboratory.

This book is what happens when you take thirty years of practice, a lifetime of teaching, real-world experience in conflict zones, and ask: what if the ancient wisdom was right all along? What if consciousness really does create reality? What if we could prove it not with instruments, but with lived experience?

the cyprus story

Summer 1995. Mediterranean heat. Twenty Greek Cypriots. Twenty Turkish Cypriots. Decades of division between them.

Our team's job: create conditions for dialogue across the Green Line—the border, the barrier, the thing that made these two communities foreign to each other despite sharing an island.

This is track two diplomacy. Not politicians. Not official negotiations. Community leaders. People with influence but not power. The ones who might actually change something because they're not bound by official positions.

I watched something extraordinary happen.

When we stopped trying to solve the problem. When we stopped pushing for resolution. When we simply created space—present, grounded, open space—people started talking. Really talking. Not arguing positions. Not defending territories.

Listening.

Once you listen, really listen, you discover what's actually going on beneath the stated conflict. You find the human beings under the positions. You locate the fear under the anger. You sense the grief under the righteousness.

Then transformation becomes possible.

Not because you had the right technique. Not because you said the right thing. Because you were present enough to let the energy move. To let it find its own resolution.

That's standing on the floating bridge of heaven. That's where 1+1=1.

this book

Quantum Aikido: The Power of Harmony releases January 6, 2026, through Inner Traditions/Park Street Press.

It's divided into two parts. First: entering the quantum world—the principles, the science, the philosophy. Second: O Sensei's process—love the mystery, breathe the harmony, joy as the way of the warrior.

Douglas Stone wrote the foreword. We met in Cyprus. He teaches at Harvard Law School, co-authored Difficult Conversations. He understood immediately what happens when you bring Aikido principles into negotiation work.

This isn't a book about martial arts, though martial arts are in it.
It isn't a book about physics, though physics is in it.
It isn't a book about meditation or business or conflict resolution, though all of those are in it.

It's a book about energy. About consciousness. About what becomes possible when you stop fighting reality and start dancing with it.

what I teach

The book is one expression. But the teaching takes many forms.

I still train people in Aikido—physical practice on the mat. I coach executives who need to transform their relationship with pressure. I work with organizations navigating change. I facilitate difficult conversations where people have forgotten how to listen.

All of it comes back to the same principles:

WIGO - What Is Really Going On beneath surface appearances

The Quantum Pause - space between stimulus and response

Observer Effect - your state determines your reality

Center and Circle - strong force and subtle force in balance

The Floating Bridge - where opposites complete each other

These aren't theories. They're practices. Ways of being. Choices you make moment by moment about where to place your attention, how to meet energy, whether to resist or harmonize.
