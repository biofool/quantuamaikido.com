Long (250 words)

In Quantum Aikido: The Power of Harmony, Richard Moon—6th Dan Aikido master, international peace negotiator, and executive coach—reveals a revolutionary understanding: the principles governing subatomic particles also govern human consciousness and conflict.

Drawing from quantum mechanics concepts like wave-particle duality, superposition, and the observer effect, Moon shows how consciousness creates reality through attention and presence. When we shift our relationship with energy—from resistance to harmony—conflict transforms from something to win or lose into something to dance with.

Moon brings thirty years of Aikido practice, real-world peace negotiation experience (including Cyprus conflict resolution), and decades of executive coaching into a comprehensive framework. Core concepts include WIGO (What Is Really Going On beneath surface appearances), the Quantum Pause (the space between stimulus and response where infinite possibility exists), and the Unified Field Experience (center and circle creating wholeness).

This isn't another wellness book offering easy answers. Moon writes for advanced moving meditation practitioners and leaders who understand that consciousness transformation isn't comfortable—it's necessary.

Published by Inner Traditions/Park Street Press in the Sacred Planet Books series, with foreword by Douglas Stone (Harvard Law School lecturer, co-author of Difficult Conversations), Quantum Aikido offers intellectually rigorous, practically applicable wisdom for transforming how we meet pressure, conflict, change, and the unknown.

Standing on the floating bridge of heaven where 1+1=1, Moon invites readers to recognize: conflict isn't something to win or lose, but energy waiting to be transformed.

Richard Moon is a 6th Dan Aikido sensei, international peace negotiator, and executive coach specializing in consciousness transformation under pressure. He trained with Robert Nadeau Shihan, who studied directly with Aikido founder Morihei Ueshiba (O Sensei).

Moon has worked in track two diplomacy, including Cyprus peace negotiations, applying quantum principles to conflict resolution. In the 1990s, he pioneered electronic coaching—delivering 60-120 second voicemail teachings to corporate executives, a format that presaged modern social media content.

He bridges quantum mechanics, Aikido philosophy, and real-world application, teaching leaders and practitioners to transform reactivity into creative response.

Long (250 words)

Richard Moon is a 6th Dan Aikido sensei whose work bridges ancient martial wisdom with modern physics, real-world conflict resolution with consciousness transformation.

He began training in Aikido under Robert Nadeau Shihan, one of the few Westerners to study directly with Morihei Ueshiba (O Sensei), founder of Aikido. O Sensei described Aikido not as a martial art but as "the realization of love"—a phrase that would come to define Moon's life work.

In the 1990s, Moon worked in international peace negotiation, including track two diplomacy efforts in Cyprus bringing together Greek and Turkish Cypriot community leaders. These experiences revealed how Aikido principles—entering, blending, harmonizing with energy—applied powerfully in high-stakes human conflict beyond the training mat.

During the same period, Moon pioneered electronic coaching for corporate executives, delivering thousands of 60-120 second voicemail teachings. This corpus of work, focused on presence under pressure and "What Is Really Going On" beneath surface appearances, perfectly anticipated modern short-form content consumption decades before YouTube Shorts or Instagram Reels existed.

Moon's background includes executive coaching, mediation, organizational facilitation, and what he calls "Extraordinary Listening"—a methodology for transforming how people meet difficulty, pressure, and the unknown.

His teaching integrates quantum mechanics concepts (wave-particle duality, observer effect, uncertainty, superposition) with Aikido practice (center, circle, blending, entering) and consciousness development.
